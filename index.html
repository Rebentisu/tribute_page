Netflix Tribute Page

A brief timeline of this notorious entertainment company

Here are the major milestones:

  • 1997 - Netflix is founded by Reed Hastings and Marc Randolph in Scotts Valley, California. It was initially a DVD-by-mail service that allowed users to rent DVDs online and have them delivered to their homes.
  • 1998 - Netflix launches its website, allowing users to rent DVDs online. The company offers a free, 7-day trial to new customers.
  • 2002 - Netflix goes public
  • 2007 - Netflix introduces its streaming service, allowing users to watch movies and TV shows instantly on their computers. The service initially offered around 1,000 titles, but has since grown to include thousands more.
  • 2010 - Netflix expands its service into Canada, marking the company's first international expansion.
  • 2013 - Netflix releases its first original series, "House of Cards." The political drama is a critical and commercial success, and helps establish Netflix as a major player in the entertainment industry.
  • 2015 - Netflix announces plans to expand its service to over 130 new countries, making it available in almost every country in the world.
  • 2017 - Netflix announces that it has surpassed 100 million subscribers worldwide, solidifying its position as the world's leading streaming service.
  • 2021 - Netflix continues to produce original content and expand its global reach, with plans to release over 70 new movies and TV shows in 2021 alone. Despite increasing competition, the company remains a dominant force in the entertainment industry.
  • 2023 - Wins six Academy Awards
  • 2023 - Enters live streaming with Chris Rock: Selective Outrage.
